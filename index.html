Camera Adversaria is a mobile app designed to disrupt the automatic surveillance of personal photographs by technology companies. It achieves this by subtly changing the pixels in your photos to confuse image classification systems.

How it works

Camera Adversaria applies a particular kind of filter, called an "adversarial perturbation", to your photos. This filter is designed to muddle the low-level pixel patterns that contemporary AI systems use to make sense of images.

When you post your images on the web or back them up on the servers of big tech companies, machine vision systems are likely being used to extract information about you and what you're photographing.
